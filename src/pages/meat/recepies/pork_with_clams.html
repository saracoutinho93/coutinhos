Meat Recepies

Portuguese Pork with Clams "Carne à Alentejana"

Ingredients

800gr of pork loin cut into small cubes
4 cloves of garlic
2 tablespoons of salted bellpeppers paste
1 tablespoon of lard / 2 tablespoons of olive oil
1 small onion, chopped
2/3 tamarillas (alternative)
1dl of white wine
1kg of clams
salt to taste
coriander
500gr diced potatoes

Directions

Grind the garlic cloves in a mortar with sea salt until an even paste. Rub the meat with the paste and the bellpeppers paste. Let it marinate for 1 to 2 hours.

Sauté the onions (and tamarillos) with the chosen fat and pour the meat into the stew. Add the coriander and white wine. Sauté over low heat for about an hour.

10 minutes before its done, add the clams and cook them until they open up.

Serve it with french fries (in cubes ) with with the meat and clams on the side or Mix it into the pan along with the rest. Add some feshly cut coriander and serve.

Story Time

This is a recepie that my mother cooks very well, it's a typical southern portuguese dish but more and more we can find it everywhere in restaurants.

The first time my mother tried this recepie it was cooked by my grandfather. My grandpa' had a Tomarillo tree and ate the bitter fruits quite often. So no wonder he added them to this dish, and it really was delicious.

If you're feeling brave i highly recomend trying it with tomarillos, if you get them of corse.
